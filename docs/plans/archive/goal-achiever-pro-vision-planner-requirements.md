# Goal Achiever Pro: Vision Planner Feature Requirements

## Document Purpose
This document provides framework context and derived feature requirements for building the Vision Planner module within Goal Achiever Pro. It's intended as a reference for developers to understand the underlying methodology and translate it into application features.

---

## Part 1: Core Frameworks

### 1.1 The Belief-Vision-Manifestation Loop

**Core Concept:**
Success requires having faith in a vision before seeing proof it will happen. The formula is:

> Faith → Belief → Vision → Action → Manifestation

Most people fail because they want proof before believing. The process works in reverse—you must believe first, then the proof follows.

**Key Principles:**
- 100% clarity on what you want
- 100% belief that it's possible
- 100% of the time (consistent focus, not occasional)
- Write it down—what isn't written won't be remembered
- Visual reinforcement matters (vision boards, wallpapers, documents)

**Real-World Application:**
A goal written down and visualized daily for years (even without immediate proof) will often manifest in unexpected ways. The act of consistent focus creates opportunities that wouldn't otherwise appear.

---

### 1.2 The Four Revenue Rhythms

**Core Concept:**
You don't get what you want—you get what you focus on. Business success comes from maintaining rhythm across four key areas:

| Rhythm | Core Question |
|--------|---------------|
| **Marketing** | Are we generating enough leads? |
| **Sales** | Are we converting opportunities? |
| **Model** | Is our business model fundamentally sound? |
| **Systems** | Do we have systems that buy back our time? |

**Key Insight:**
Most business problems aren't marketing or sales issues—they're broken business models. People often resist changing their model because they're emotionally attached to how they've always done things.

---

### 1.3 The Achievement System (Standards Over Goals)

**Core Concept:**
You don't rise to the level of your goals—you fall to the level of your standards/systems.

**The Difference:**
- **Goals** = Desired outcomes (easy to ignore)
- **Standards** = Non-negotiable daily behaviors (what actually drives results)

**Key Principles:**
- Focus on the *preceding activities* that make goals inevitable
- If you hold a standard around activity, outcomes become predictable
- Review goals multiple times per day (e.g., 3x daily)
- Build in reward systems tied to standards, not just outcomes

**Example:**
Instead of "I want to run a marathon," the standard becomes "I run 5 days per week, minimum 30 minutes." The marathon becomes inevitable.

---

### 1.4 The Five Daily Non-Negotiables

**Core Concept:**
A set of 5 daily behaviors that create your "personal economy"—your baseline operating system for success.

**Three Outcomes of Daily Non-Negotiables:**
1. **Direct business growth** - More customers, more revenue
2. **Culture & attraction** - Better talent, partnerships, and team culture
3. **Personal transformation** - You become the person capable of achieving bigger goals

**Key Insight:**
Resistance to daily disciplines often comes from making it about yourself ("What if I look bad?"). The shift is to make it about serving others.

**The Capacity Principle:**
> "The extremities expand the capacities."

Meaning: Pushing beyond comfort zones builds capacity to operate at higher levels. What feels impossible today becomes easy once capacity is built.

---

### 1.5 The Audience Growth Engine

**Core Concept:**
An infinity loop between two activities:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                         │
│   SOCIAL MEDIA  ←────────→  STAGES      │
│   (Internal)                (External)  │
│                                         │
│   • Build following        • Podcasts   │
│   • Conversations          • Speaking   │
│   • Revenue capture        • Webinars   │
│                            • Collabs    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**The Process:**
1. Create content for your ideal customers
2. People follow you because they value the content
3. Have conversations with followers (engagement)
4. Make offers to those who are a fit
5. Get on other people's stages to expand reach
6. Repeat

**Common Failures:**
- Creating content but never talking to followers
- Having followers but never making offers
- Making offers but not getting on external stages for new eyeballs

---

### 1.6 The 10x Vision Framework

**Core Concept:**
Your vision should be described with the same specificity as your current reality. If you can't describe your future life in vivid detail, you don't actually have a vision.

**Components:**
- **Vivid Vision Document** - A written, detailed description of your life/business 3 years out
- **Vision Board** - Visual representation placed where you see it constantly (wallpaper, office wall)
- **Regular Updates** - Revise every 3 years or as goals are achieved

**The Test:**
Can you describe your future with as much detail as you can describe today? That's the level of clarity required.

---

### 1.7 The Mindset Shifts

**Shift 1: From Self-Focus to Service**
When you stop making it about your revenue, your fears, your image—and start making it about how you can serve others—everything shifts. Energy changes, opportunities appear, people want to support you.

**Shift 2: From Passive to Active Belief**
Treat every potential customer as someone who WILL buy. Belief influences behavior, which influences outcomes.

**Shift 3: From Complexity to Simplicity**
> "It's easy to create complex things. It's hard to create simple things."

The most effective systems are deceptively simple. Resist the urge to overcomplicate.

## Part 3: UX Considerations

### 3.1 Core Principles

1. **Simplicity over complexity** - Resist feature bloat. The system should feel simple even if powerful underneath.

2. **Visibility of vision** - The user's vision/goals should be ever-present, not buried in menus.

3. **Daily engagement focus** - The app should support daily use without feeling burdensome.

4. **Progress celebration** - Build in moments of recognition for consistency and achievement.

5. **Accountability without shame** - Track streaks and progress without making users feel bad for missed days.

### 3.2 Key User Flows

**Flow 1: New User Onboarding**
1. Welcome + philosophy overview
2. Guided vision creation (can be brief initially)
3. Set 3-5 initial goals
4. Define first set of daily non-negotiables
5. Set review reminder preferences

**Flow 2: Daily Use**
1. Morning: Review vision/goals, see today's non-negotiables
2. Throughout day: Mark non-negotiables complete as done
3. Evening: Quick reflection, celebrate wins

**Flow 3: Weekly Review**
1. Review goal progress
2. Check non-negotiable adherence %
3. Review business rhythms (if applicable)
4. Adjust/recommit for coming week
