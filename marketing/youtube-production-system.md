## YouTube Algorithm Playbook

### How YouTube Decides What to Recommend

```
The algorithm optimizes for ONE thing: Session Time
↓
Videos that keep people on YouTube longer get promoted more
↓
Your goal: Make videos people click AND watch
```

### The 4 Metrics That Matter Most

| Metric | What It Is | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|-----------|--------|----------------|
| **CTR** | Click-through rate (thumbnail + title) | >5% (aim 8-12%) | Gets you into the game |
| **AVD** | Average view duration | >50% of video length | Proves quality |
| **Retention** | Where people stop watching | No major drop-offs | Shows engagement |
| **Session Time** | Do viewers watch MORE after your video? | Positive session | Algorithm gold |

### The Packaging Formula

```
GREAT VIDEO = Great Idea + Great Packaging + Great Execution

Great Idea: Topic people actively want to watch
Great Packaging: Thumbnail + Title that compels clicks
Great Execution: Delivery that holds attention

Most creators fail at PACKAGING, not content.
```

### Topic Selection Matrix

```
                    HIGH SEARCH VOLUME
                           │
         ┌─────────────────┼─────────────────┐
         │   DISCOVERY     │   GOLDMINE      │
         │   ZONE          │   ZONE          │
         │                 │                 │
         │   Hard to rank  │   High demand   │

         │                 │                 │

```

### Video Types by Algorithm Behavior

| Type | Algorithm Behavior | Best For | Shelf Life |
|------|-------------------|----------|------------|
| **Tutorial/How-To** | Search + suggested | Building authority | Evergreen (years) |

## Viral Hook Formulas

### The First 3 Seconds

```
PATTERN INTERRUPT → CURIOSITY GAP → PROMISE

Pattern Interrupt: Something unexpected that stops the scroll
Curiosity Gap: Opens a loop the viewer must close
Promise: What they'll get if they keep watching
```

### 15 Proven Hook Templates

#### Category: Contrarian/Provocative
```
1. "Everything you've been told about [topic] is wrong."
   Example: "Everything you've been told about goal setting is wrong."

2. "[Common advice] is actually killing your [desired outcome]."
   Example: "To-do lists are actually killing your productivity."

3. "Stop [common behavior]. Here's what to do instead."
   Example: "Stop setting annual goals. Here's what to do instead."
```

#### Category: Curiosity/Mystery
```
4. "I discovered something that changed everything about [topic]."
   Example: "I discovered something that changed everything about how I use my time."

5. "The [unexpected thing] that [impressive result]."
   Example: "The 15-minute habit that 10x'd my productivity."

6. "What [successful person/group] knows about [topic] that you don't."
   Example: "What 7-figure entrepreneurs know about time that you don't."
```

#### Category: Result/Transformation
```
7. "How I [achieved result] in [timeframe] (and you can too)."
   Example: "How I reclaimed 20 hours per week in 30 days."

8. "I went from [bad state] to [good state]. Here's the system."
   Example: "I went from 70-hour weeks to 40 with more revenue. Here's the system."

9. "[Number]% of people fail at [thing]. Here's why you won't."
   Example: "93% of goals fail. Here's why yours won't."
```

#### Category: Story/Narrative
```
10. "I tracked [thing] for [time]. What I found will shock you."
    Example: "I tracked my time for 2 weeks. What I found was embarrassing."

11. "The [mistake] that cost me [consequence]."
    Example: "The $50,000 mistake that taught me delegation."

12. "After [experience], I'll never [behavior] again."
    Example: "After burning out twice, I'll never ignore this signal again."
```

#### Category: Practical/Valuable
```
13. "The exact [system/framework] I use to [result]."
    Example: "The exact system I use to achieve any goal I set."

14. "[Number] [things] that will [transformation]."
    Example: "3 rituals that will transform your mornings."

15. "The only [number] things you need to [outcome]."
    Example: "The only 3 things you need to track for productivity."
```

### Hook Amplifiers

Add these to increase hook power:

```
Time Pressure: "...before it's too late"
Specificity: "...specifically for entrepreneurs"
Social Proof: "...that top performers use"
Exclusivity: "...that nobody talks about"
Simplicity: "...and it only takes 5 minutes"
Contrast: "...while working less"
```

### Hook Testing Protocol

```
For every video, write 5 hooks.
Test with trusted group or A/B thumbnail.
Track which style performs best.
Double down on winning formulas.
```

## Retention Engineering

### The Retention Graph Anatomy

```
100% ┌──────────────────────────────────────
     │ ╲
     │  ╲ ← INITIAL DROP (0-30 sec)
     │   ╲   If this is steep, fix your hook
     │    ────────────────────────
     │                           ╲
     │     ← CONTENT ZONE         ╲
     │       Should be flat        ╲
     │       or gradual decline     ╲
     │                               ╲
  0% └──────────────────────────────────────
     0 sec                        End
```

### Retention Strategies by Section

#### Opening (0-30 seconds)
```
PROBLEMS:
- Slow logo/intro
- "Hey guys, welcome to my channel"
- Long setup before value

SOLUTIONS:
- Start mid-action or mid-sentence
- Hook immediately (see formulas above)
- Promise specific value in first 10 seconds
- No intro music/logos (or <3 seconds)
```

#### Body (30 sec - 80% of video)
```
PROBLEMS:
- Long monologues without visual change
- Tangents that don't serve the video
- Predictable structure
- No pattern interrupts

SOLUTIONS:
- Change visuals every 5-10 seconds
- B-roll, text, graphics, camera changes
- Open curiosity loops throughout
- "But here's where it gets interesting..."
- Use "bucket brigades" (transitional phrases)
```

#### Closing (last 20%)
```
PROBLEMS:
- "That's it for today"
- Obvious wrap-up signals
- Begging for engagement

SOLUTIONS:
- Save a key insight for the end
- Open loop to next video
- Strong, confident CTA
- End screen while still delivering value
```

### Bucket Brigade Phrases

Use these to keep viewers watching:

```
CURIOSITY DRIVERS:
"Here's where it gets interesting..."
"But there's a problem..."
"Now, most people get this wrong..."
"Let me show you what I mean..."
"This is the part nobody talks about..."

PATTERN INTERRUPTS:
"Quick sidebar..."
"Real talk for a second..."
"I have to be honest..."
"Plot twist..."
"Wait, it gets better..."

ENGAGEMENT HOOKS:
"Pay attention to this part..."
"This is the most important thing..."
"If you remember nothing else..."
"Bookmark this..."
"Screenshot this..."
```

### Re-engagement Triggers

```
Every 2-3 minutes, include:
□ A question (rhetorical or direct)
□ A visual change (B-roll, graphic, camera)
□ A new curiosity loop opened
□ A mini-payoff from earlier setup
□ Energy shift (pace, volume, intensity)
```

### The 10-Second Rule

```
Viewers decide to stay or leave every 10 seconds.
↓
Every 10 seconds, ask:
"Is there a reason to keep watching RIGHT NOW?"
↓
If no: Add B-roll, text, open a loop, change energy
```

## Content Flywheel

### The 1-7-30 Content System

```
1 LONG-FORM VIDEO (10-20 min)
        │
        ├──→ 7 SHORTS (clips + original)
        │
        ├──→ 30 SOCIAL POSTS (carousel, tweets, stories)
        │
        └──→ 1 BLOG POST (SEO)

Every piece points back to the long-form or to the app.
```

### Repurposing Workflow

```
Step 1: Record long-form with Shorts in mind
        - Include quotable moments
        - Film vertical B-roll
        - Pause at key points for clean cuts

Step 2: Edit long-form first

Step 3: Extract Shorts
        - Best hook → Short 1
        - Key insight → Short 2
        - Actionable tip → Short 3
        - Controversial take → Short 4
        - Before/after → Short 5
        - Quick tutorial → Short 6
        - Behind the scenes → Short 7

Step 4: Create social content
        - Key quotes → Twitter threads
        - Steps → Instagram carousels
        - Stats → LinkedIn posts

Step 5: Schedule everything
        - Long-form: Wednesday 10am
        - Shorts: Daily or every other day
        - Social: Throughout the week
```

## Shorts Strategy Deep Dive

### Shorts vs Long-Form Relationship

```
SHORTS DO:
✓ Drive massive reach and impressions
✓ Bring new subscribers
✓ Test video ideas quickly
✓ Build brand familiarity

SHORTS DON'T:
✗ Generate much ad revenue
✗ Build deep relationships
✗ Convert to product sales directly
✗ Teach complex material

STRATEGY:
Shorts = top of funnel (awareness)
Long-form = middle of funnel (trust)
CTA/App = bottom of funnel (conversion)
```

### Shorts Viral Patterns

```
Pattern 1: TRANSFORMATION
"Watch this [before] become [after]"
Works for: Systems, routines, results

Pattern 2: REVEAL
"I found [surprising thing]"
Works for: Time audits, data, insights

Pattern 3: CONTROVERSY
"[Popular advice] is wrong because..."
Works for: Contrarian takes, mythbusting

Pattern 4: RAPID VALUE
"[Number] [things] in [time]"
Works for: Tips, hacks, lists

Pattern 5: STORY
"I [did thing]. Here's what happened."
Works for: Personal experiences, experiments
```

### Shorts Posting Strategy

```
Frequency: 1-2 per day (minimum 5/week)
Best times: Test, but generally:
  - 7-9am local
  - 12-2pm local
  - 7-9pm local

Mix:
- 50% clips from long-form
- 30% original Short ideas
- 20% trending sounds/formats adapted
```
