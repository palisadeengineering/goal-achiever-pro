## Tweet 2 (The Truth)
The truth about time:

You have the same 168 hours as everyone else.

Elon Musk. Your competitor. That productive friend.

It's awareness of where it goes.

---

## Tweet 3 (The Method)
The 15-minute method:

For 7 days, log every 15-minute block:
- What you did
- Category (work, personal, admin, waste)
- Energy level (1-5)

Yes, it's tedious.
Yes, it's worth it.
One week changes everything.

---

## Tweet 4 (Setup)
Simple setup (use paper or app):

| Time | Activity | Category | Energy |
|------|----------|----------|--------|
| 8:00 | Emails | Admin | 3 |
| 8:15 | Emails | Admin | 2 |
| 8:30 | Meeting | Work | 4 |

Every 15 minutes. No exceptions.

---

## Tweet 5 (Categories)
Categorize everything:

WORK - Revenue-generating activities
ADMIN - Necessary but not productive
PERSONAL - Health, relationships, growth
BUFFER - Transitions, breaks
WASTE - Scrolling, rabbit holes, procrastination

Be honest with yourself.

---

## Tweet 6 (Day 1-2)
Days 1-2 reality check:

You'll discover:
- Email takes 2x longer than you thought
- Meetings eat 30%+ of your week
- "Quick" social media = 1-2 hours daily
- Context switching is killing you

Don't change anything yet. Just observe.

---

## Tweet 7 (Day 3-4)
Days 3-4 patterns emerge:

Look for:
- Peak energy windows (when do you feel best?)
- Energy vampires (what drains you?)
- Time black holes (where does time disappear?)
- Productive streaks (when do you flow?)

---

## Tweet 8 (Day 5-7)
Days 5-7 deep analysis:

Calculate your actual allocation:
- Hours on high-value work: ___
- Hours on admin/low-value: ___
- Hours wasted: ___
- Hours on health/personal: ___

Compare to your ideal. Feel the gap.

## Tweet 9 (My Results)
My time audit results:

BEFORE:
- High-value work: 18 hours
- Admin/emails: 22 hours
- Meetings: 15 hours
- Waste: 12 hours

Painful to see in writing.

## Tweet 10 (The Fixes)
What I changed:

1. Batched email to 2x/day (saved 8 hours)
2. Cut meetings by 50% (saved 7 hours)
3. Blocked social media during work (saved 6 hours)
4. Delegated admin tasks (saved 5 hours)

Total reclaimed: 26 hours/week

---

## Tweet 11 (Energy Insight)
The energy revelation:

My peak hours: 8-11 AM
What I was doing then: Emails and meetings

Complete waste of my best energy.

Now: Deep work 8-11 AM, no exceptions.
Emails and meetings after 2 PM.

Game changer.

---

## Tweet 12 (Time Vampires)
Common time vampires to watch for:

1. "Quick" email checks (15 min each x 10 = 2.5 hours)
2. Context switching (20 min to refocus each time)
3. Unnecessary meetings (could be emails)
4. Decision fatigue (small choices drain energy)
5. Social media "breaks" (never just 5 minutes)

---

## Tweet 13 (Protection)
Protect your findings:

1. Block your peak hours for deep work
2. Batch low-energy tasks together
3. Create "do not disturb" windows
4. Schedule email checking (not constant)
5. Audit again in 30 days

What gets measured gets managed.
